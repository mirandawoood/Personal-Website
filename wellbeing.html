Organisation. Doesn't mean being cruel to yourself.

I maintain that only planning what we are obligated to do (but don't want to do) will result in us getting less done and in feelings of frustration, resentment and the desire to give up. I have got into the habit of planning my day including my free time and evenings. I divide my days into3 parts...Work: be it school, university or a job it feels like some kind of work takes up almost all of our lives. Domestics: Washing up, laundry, cleaning or food shopping. FUN... what we feel we never have enough time for. My sister recently told me something simple and obvious which somehow still astonished me. There are twenty-four hours in a day which means you have eight for sleep, eight for work, and eight whole hours a day to yourself. Admittedly it is important to keep up with household stuff, but if we alot an hour or so a day for it, we can have more time for ourselves than we think possible. Essentially planning your free time is a vital part of enjoying it.
