Journeys with ‘The Waste Land’ Sat 3 Feb - Mon 7 May 2018 “On Margate Sands. I can connect. Nothing with nothing.” (T.S. Eliot, The Waste Land)

Margate's Turner Contemporary offers an exibition in homage to Eliot's time spent there after the traumas of First World War. It was there that he pieced together the framents of his arguably most famous poem 'The Waste Land'. The frangmentory nature of the poem is explored through a huge variation of works from different artists. From the etherial and technical beauty of Turner's 'The Golden Bough' to Paula Rego's series of paintings on abortion, the exibition not only allows us consideration of the simoultaniously fragmentary and fluid forces of Eliot's poems, but also of the more contemporary understandings of what it means to exist in a world where violence, fear and war are still prevailant.

Oh yeah, it's that good. See for yourself.
